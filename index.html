Common questions

The stuff owners actually ask us.

Pulled from real first calls. If you've got something we haven't covered, ask on the call — or email Ricky.

How is this different from just using ChatGPT? +

ChatGPT is a chat window. You have to remember to open it, paste in context, and ask the right question. The tools we build are integrated — they live inside the workflows you already have. A new lead comes in, the tool answers it. A receipt hits your inbox, the tool categorizes it. You don't open anything. You don't prompt anything. It just runs.

Do I need someone technical on my team to run it? +

No. We design every build so a non-technical owner can use it day-to-day. If anything needs attention, it sends you a Slack message or text. We also record a walkthrough video so anyone on your team — even someone hired six months from now — can learn it in 15 minutes.

What happens if it breaks or makes a mistake? +

Every build has approval gates wherever the stakes are real — sending a customer message, processing a payment, booking a job. The tool drafts; you approve. For the first 30 days after launch, we monitor it daily and fix anything that drifts. After that, you can keep us on retainer (from $750/mo) or take it fully in-house — your call.

Is my customer data safe? +

Short answer: yes. Your data stays in your tools (QuickBooks, your CRM, your inbox). We don't move it to some shared platform. The AI providers we use (OpenAI, Anthropic) have business-grade contracts that prohibit training on your data. We sign an NDA before we start, and we're happy to walk through the data flow on the first call.

What if AI isn't the right answer for my problem? +

We'll tell you. Honestly — most of our first calls end with us saying "you don't need AI for that, you need a better process" or "this is a $200 Zapier zap, not a $5k build." We make our money on great fits, not by selling everyone an automation. The 15-minute call is free either way.

Do I have to switch any of the tools I'm already using? +

Almost never. We build on top of what you already use — QuickBooks, ServiceTitan, Jobber, HubSpot, Google Workspace, Outlook, whatever. The goal is to make your existing stack smarter, not give you another platform to learn.

How long does it actually take? +

Most first projects: 1–2 weeks from kickoff to live. Bigger bundles: 2–3 weeks. You'll see progress every few days, not at the end. We don't disappear into a six-month build cycle.

What if I want to cancel or change directions? +

Project work is paid 50% up front, 50% on delivery — no long contracts. If you go on retainer afterwards, you can cancel anytime with 30 days' notice. You always own whatever we've built for you.
